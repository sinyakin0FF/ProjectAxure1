Dermatology is one of the few medical fields where life-threatening diseases can often be diagnosed with the naked eye. A single mole can tell a story about a person’s immune system, genetics, and lifestyle. The skin is the largest organ of the human body, and many internal disorders — from diabetes to liver disease — first manifest on the skin. Dermato-oncology has become a rapidly growing field in the Czech Republic, thanks to advanced imaging tools like digital dermatoscopy and AI-assisted diagnostics.

Professional focus

Petr Svoboda focuses on early detection, surgical removal, and follow-up treatment of malignant and pre-malignant skin lesions. He is also deeply involved in melanoma prevention programs and public education about UV protection. His main interest lies in combining classical dermatologic surgery with modern diagnostic imaging to improve patient outcomes.

About

Dr. Svoboda is a meticulous and research-oriented dermatologist with over nine years of experience in clinical and oncologic dermatology. His patients appreciate his calm demeanor and ability to explain complex medical topics in simple terms. Outside of work, he enjoys hiking and photography — passions that, as he says, “help him see details others might miss,” both in nature and in his medical practice.
